RESOURCES

UNC Resources

UNC Chapel Hill offers many effective mental health and wellness resources for students.
Learn more about them below.

Counseling and Psychological Services (CAPS)

CAPS at UNC is a great resource for students looking for one-on-one support with a therapist or peer support with group therapy. Anyone can use CAPS resources, regardless of demographic or whether you have a diagnosis. It is a confidential service with no session limits. For more information, click the link above.

Student Wellness

Student Wellness provides plenty of resources for students looking for ways to maintain a healthy, balanced lifestyle. We recommend their self care program, which includes strategies and advice for stress management, healthy relationships, rest, and mindfulness.

Campus Health

Students can make appointments with Campus Health for various medical concerns, including mental health concerns. If you have any concerns about your health, please contact Campus Health for help and, if necessary, Accessibility Resources and Service (ARS) for academic accomodations.

Additional Resources

Self Care

What is self-care? Self care means looking after yourself physically, mentally, and emotionally through stress-relieving activities and prioritizing and validating your needs.


Some self-care activities include:

  • drawing
  • yoga and meditation
  • cleaning
  • gardening
  • listening to relaxing music

Other Recommendations

We also recommend reading books and listening to podcasts on mental health and self-care, such as Emotional First Aid by Guy Winch, The Positive Psychology Podcast, and Active Minds at Carolina Podcast. It is also a good idea to talk with trusted figures, such as close friends, family, and mentors, about your mental health.


Remember to take breaks, stay hydrated, and get enough sleep. Healthy nutrition and fitness habits are also beneficial to mental health. Know that it's always okay to seek help, whether it's through family, a professional, or an organization like Active Minds.
